The Problem

Cooling demand is rising. The tools to manage it aren’t keeping up.

Three independent sources shaped our focus. Each points to the same gap: cooling load is growing fast, grid stress is already measurable, and the software-level controls that could reduce it remain underdeployed.

What We Built

A sensor-to-dashboard pipeline for adaptive energy monitoring

Instead of simulating, we instrumented a real device. The stack captures temperature every 10 seconds, logs mode and estimated wattage to the cloud, and surfaces energy comparisons with live outdoor weather context — all in one connected pipeline.

Embedded telemetry

The ESP32 reads indoor temperature every 10 seconds, maps it to a fan mode (OFF / LOW / MED / HIGH), and posts estimated wattage and mode data to Supabase.

Live weather context

The Streamlit dashboard fetches current and peak outdoor temperature from Open-Meteo to frame adaptive comparison thresholds and savings calculations.

Energy analytics

Streamlit turns raw telemetry into time-weighted kWh, baseline comparisons, cost estimates across electricity markets, mode distribution, and live status cards.

How It Works

A four-layer prototype

The build is centered on visibility and comparison. The ESP32 gathers and publishes readings, Supabase stores the time-series data, Streamlit turns that data into a live dashboard, and Open-Meteo adds outdoor weather context.

  • ESP32 firmware: reads indoor temperature and maps mode plus estimated wattage.
  • Supabase: stores device_id, temp_c, power_w, and fan_mode in fan_readings.
  • Streamlit dashboard: computes weather thresholds, rolling analytics, savings, and cost.
  • Open-Meteo: supplies the current and peak outdoor temperature used in the dashboard.

Current Scope

What the prototype is and what it is not

What it is

A working firmware-to-dashboard pipeline for a fan-focused case study, with live telemetry, weather framing, and energy comparison views.

What it is not yet

A direct weather-actuated hardware controller. The ESP32 still uses fixed indoor temperature bands rather than live outdoor weather on-device.

What comes next

Relay or PWM-based control, hysteresis logic, multi-device support, and direct power measurement are natural extensions from the current build.
